Our favorite food


In a city where finance and shipping industry our known worldwide, food is the backbone and obsession of many Hong Kong residents. Located in a strategic position and with a unique history of foreign and colonial influence, Hong Kong's food scene is diverse and always exciting. One of the reasons you should travel to Hong Kong is to try their local food.

Siu Mei,

is known as the Chinese barbecue with roast-flavored meats.


Ordering take away char siu with steam rice for dinner...

Cantonese are experts in roasting meats. They have unbelievable selection of roast pork, chicken, char siu (barbecued pork) and barbecued goose. The roast is usually coated with a flavoredful sauce (a different sauce is used for each variety of meat) before roasting. It can be served with steam rice or noodles. Chinese barbecue is purchased as take away and this kind of street food restaurants are almost everywhere. It is impossible to miss this cuisine in Hong Kong!

Hot Pot,

is a Chinese cooking method, prepared with a sinnering pot of soup and various ingredients.


Hot pot with friends on a Friday night be like...

Hot pot is a form of communal dining where people gather around a big pot of boiling broth on a single burner and cook their own meats and vegetables. It is like an Asian fondue, but rather than dipping cubes of bread in cheese we're swishing slices of fatty beef in delicious broth. There are two kinds of hot pot soup: spicy and coconut milk. Typical hot pot dishes include thinly sliced meat (beef, lamb, goat), leaf vegetables, mushrooms, different kind of dumplings, tofu and seafood (prawns, clams, squid,...) and more. There are plenty of restaurants specialized in hot pot, but it's easy to make your own for a party or Sunday dinner.

Dim Sum,

are usually steamed dishes but you can also find baked or fried food.


Eating dim sum for the first time with local friends...

There is an ancient Chinese tradition of drinking tea, known as yum cha, and with tea it's common to eat little bite sizes dishes which are known as dim sum. That's why dim sum is often served at teahouses and always goes with hot tea. This is one of the most fun and delicious food experiences you can have.

Chinese noodles,

are an essential ingredient in Chinese cuisine.


Eating a hot noodle soup with 32 degrees outside...

We tried different kinds of noodles (egg noodles, rice noodles,...) in traditional restaurants. Besides beef balls and fish balls, we can also choose dumplings, beef tenderloin, deep-fried fish skin and more. The fish balls are made from mince fish and boiled in a soupy broth.
